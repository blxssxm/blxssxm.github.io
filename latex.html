Learning LaTeX

What is LaTeX

LaTeX is for typesetting, it is incredibly robust and versatile - capable of producing: ornate documents, technical and scientific documentation, spreadsheets and provides comfortable formatting for basic homework. LaTeX is and will always be free software that is open-source.

Should you use it over traditional word processors?

A description alone won't convince people to learn LaTeX or switch over, if you are interested in efficient formatting and wish to compile .pdfs offline without being reliant on big tech such as MS Office or Docs (or even Libre Office), I would recommend creating an overleaf account or to watch the Youtuber Luke Smith and learn the syntax of LaTeX. I have used MS Office growing up, later adamantly swearing by Google Docs primarly due to it being free, I have come to my own rational conclusion that LaTeX is far superior. Word Processors are finicky and frustrating to use, simple things such as inserting a bullet point or changing the margins requires the movement of the mouse.

Packages I use(Press or hover over package names)

  1. \lettrine
    The lettrine package can be used to typeset dropcaps into articles of text, it is capable of displaying a wide variety of different dropcap desingns from Historical/Flourished, Modern/Newspaper etc.
  2. \xunicode
  3. \xpinyin
    The xpinyin package is used to display phonetic pinyin symbols with ease, to output desired tone choose numbers from 1-4 after said word
  4. \xeCJK
  5. \fancyhdr
  6. \chemfig
    Chemistry, is my weakest of sciences but this displays formulas and stuff
  7. \ruby
    The ruby package can be used to display smaller text directly above chosen text
